My Life at 27

For 27 years, I've been like a leaf blowing in the wind - going wherever life takes me, never making real choices. Sometimes I'd think, "I should take control," but the feeling always faded.

Then I discovered AI. First ChatGPT, then DeepSeek. Seeing how these systems "think" amazed me. They weren't perfect, but they tried - they processed information, they learned. And that's when it hit me:

These smart machines were made by humans. Humans who decided to create, to build, to think. But me? I was just watching life go by.

Now, for the first time, I feel excited about something - technology. I want to learn web development, even if people say I'm too old or it's too late. Maybe I'll even work with AI someday.

The difference? This time, I'm not just drifting. This time, I'm choosing.
